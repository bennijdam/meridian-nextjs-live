## Quarterly content refreshes

Every quarter, update the following:
- [ ] Date stamps on all blog posts (add "Updated: Q[X] 2026" line)
- [ ] Pricing comparisons in cost pages (validate vs competitor benchmarks)
- [ ] Stats in market report (re-check Ahrefs, SEMrush, Google Trends data)
- [ ] Review pages with <3% conversion rate after 100+ visits — rewrite or retire
- [ ] Internal linking audit — ensure every new page is linked from 3+ existing pages

## Content pillars

Every blog post must fit one of these pillars:

1. **Pricing transparency** — cost guides, market benchmarks (supports commercial intent)
2. **AI search disruption** — GEO, AEO, AI Overviews (thought leadership)
3. **London-specific insights** — borough data, local regulations, UK market (differentiator)
4. **Platform/stack comparisons** — X vs Y for honest decision-making (commercial investigation)
5. **Tactical playbooks** — step-by-step how-to content (educational, AI-citable)

## Case study cadence

Case studies are the single highest-converting content type. Rules:

- Every new client engagement starts a case study draft in month 1
- Case studies go live 6 months after kickoff (when results are demonstrable)
- Each case study must include: client name (with permission), service scope, before/after metrics (revenue, traffic, ranking, CAC), 30-day/90-day/180-day results, client quote
- Hero metric must be verifiable (screenshot, public data, or client attestation)
- Link from: homepage, service page, relevant industry page, relevant borough page

## Market report updates

- Annual refresh in Q1 (April publication window)
- Quarterly "stats refresh" — update data points without rewriting sections
- When Google announces algorithm updates or new AI Overview features, add an addendum
